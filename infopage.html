RPG: Short for Roleplaying Game

RPG is a acronym, short for Roleplaying Game. Chances are you heard of them before. Either as a genre of videogames, or maybe you heard about Dungeon and Dragons. A roleplaying game is a form of collaborative storytelling and game that generally is run by throwing dice to determine certein outcomes within the narrative. Some games use other engines of chance, such as a deck of cards rather then dice. But dice are by far the most common. The charm and appeal of a roleplaying game lies in the fact that anyone with a smidge of imagination can play it. All you need is a couple of friends, some dice and whatever basic ruleset is needed to understand the game. Depending on the game, the complexity of the game varies. Some are very rules light, others are heavy on the rules. Some emphesize combat, and making the player feel like a badass. Others focus on charachter development and social interactions. There is a myriad of games out there. All with their own sping on things. Click the example button below for an example of one such games combat.

Systems and Settings. Narrative vs Tactical

There are many different systems and settings out there. The hobby is big and ever growing more popular. More and more, Roleplaying games are hitting the mainstream. Chances are that you allready heard about Dungeons and Dragons. By far the largest brand, its far from alone or unique. There is a vibrant community out there all playing different games. Depending on what speaks to you, there is everything from dark, bloody vampire games to light hearted games meant to play with your significant other.

1) You roll as many number of die your swordplay or brawling skill allow. And you hit, you add your weapons damage to the number of successes. 2) A hit that exceeds the enemys armor score, does damage equal to remaining total 3) That total is deducted from the opponents health pool, or HP.
